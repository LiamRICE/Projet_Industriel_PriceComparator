20 April 2022
 
Call for Code projects have not slowed down since the 2021 Global Challenge winning team Saaf water was announced in November! While we are preparing to launch the 2022 Global Challenge the last week of April, Call for Code has also spent the last several months hard at work bringing tech for good open source solutions to communities where they can make a demonstrable difference.

Saaf water completes successful solution test
Saaf water, a team of India-based university students, recently completed a successful test of its accessible water quality sensor and analytics platform at a housing complex in Goa, India. The test helped Saaf water analyze the water quality of the building and they were able to update their installation process for future tests.

Chelsea Clinton and Call for Code together at SXSW EDU Austin
From 11 March through 20 March, creative industries from around the world converged in Austin, Texas for SXSW 2022, which features a variety of tracks focused on important breakthroughs in technology, film, culture, and music. This year, Bob Lord, Senior Vice President, Weather Company & Alliances and Chelsea Clinton, Clinton Foundation Vice Chair, hosted a panel with Call for Code winner Agrolly and the Call for Code for Racial Justice/YMCA - Los Angeles Five Fifths Voter app representatives to discuss how student participation is crucial to the success of finding practical solutions to the world's most pressing issues, from climate change to racial injustice.

Call for Code and AgStack open-source Ag Recommendations
Through the Call for Code ecosystem, our team of IBM Developers began working with Clemson University, who recently joined the AgStack community, to help the university digitize its vast amount of agriculture data. Through this partnership, IBM open-sourced an API for agriculture recommendations. This collaboration with Clemson University and other members of AgStack allows developers to contribute code and offer technical design input that can help any agriculture stakeholder, from rural farmers to everyday gardeners. 
 
Read the blog post to learn more


Call for Code at the University of Alabama: UA Innovate | Innovation and Hackathon Challenge
The University of Alabama hosted a women-powered hackathon from 2 April through 3 April 2022. This free digital conference provided an opportunity to hear from leading female data scientists. This was the first hackathon and innovation challenge organized by the UA MIS program and the Call for Code team facilitated the Data Analytics track on energy efficiency.
